Vin's Bits of life

A short biography about Cervin Coronacion

Childhood Years

     One thing about me is I am an alien. I was born in Saudi Arabia, where my toddler days began before eventually moving back to the Philippines. I grew up in Cabanatuan City, a place that became my home for many years and played a huge role in shaping my values and personality. My childhood was pretty much just me, as both of my parents work abroad so I had no one to play with. I was raised by my lola until my mother went home by 4th grade to finally focus on me. I was a very shy introvert kid back then who barely had any friends. But if you were to talk to my younger self, he would probably be less annoying like I am today.

Teenage Years

     If I would describe myself, I'd probably say I am both a speaker and a listener. I listen when I need to learn, when I need to adapt, when I need to hear new information or when I have to know the situation. And when I have finally gained knowledge, that is when I speak. I tend to hold myself back when I want to say something, that is my sticker mindset kicking in again. As a teen, I was passionate for the arts. I am very creative, with my skills mostly revolving around visual arts, singing, composing, and dancing. I know how to use Ibis Paint for my digital illustrations, and I also enjoy using Canva. I also have a few hobbies like crocheting and photography. I was on the search for my identity, of who I am as a person, an artist, individual. I took STEM as my senior high School strand because most of my friends were there. I learned a lot and made me a tougher student. Those times was one of the most happiest I have been.

College Years

     It has been 19 years since I came into existence, and I am currently in a program nobody expected me to take. Who would have thought that I would be studying 167 km away from home? In the heart of U-belt, the University of the East. Well, I never thought this will be my future growing up, But I ended up here because I finally went outside the box. Now, I am learning to adapt new skills for my future work, and I try to improve even if it takes time, because progress is still progress no matter how big or small.I want to be able to make my college life memorable, fun, but still full of learning, growth, and opportunities.
